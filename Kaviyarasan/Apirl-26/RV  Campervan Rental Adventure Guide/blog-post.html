10 Must-Visit National Parks This Summer

Sarah Jenkins May 10, 2026 • 5 min read

Planning a summer RV trip opens up endless possibilities, but National Parks remain the crown jewels of American road travel. From towering granite walls to geothermal wonders, these parks offer landscapes perfectly suited for campervan exploration.

1. Yosemite National Park, California

With its high concentration of waterfalls and dramatic cliffs, Yosemite is a classic. Booking an RV spot in the valley requires planning months in advance, but the view of El Capitan from your camp chair is worth every ounce of effort.

Yosemite Image

2. Grand Canyon, Arizona

Whether you stick to the developed South Rim or brave the winding roads to the North Rim, arriving at the canyon edge in a campervan is an experience unlike any other.

"There is nothing more liberating than waking up, opening the back doors of your van, and having the canyon as your front yard."

Remember to always practice Leave No Trace principles, especially when boon-docking near park borders.

Sarah Jenkins

Sarah is the Co-Founder of Wanderlust and an avid rock climber who has spent over 500 nights in a campervan across North America.
