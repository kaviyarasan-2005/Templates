The Antiquarian Book Market in 2026: A 12-Month Overview

Auctions & Market 11 April 2026 8 min read

A Year of Resilient Growth

The rare book market confounded sceptics in 2025, delivering a headline growth of 14% in auction realised values — outperforming contemporary art, decorative arts, and most alternative asset classes. This article synthesises data from Christie's, Sotheby's, and Bonhams alongside our own acquisition records to present a comprehensive market overview.

Demand Drivers

Three categories dominated growth: first edition English literature (up 22%), Islamic calligraphic manuscripts (up 31%), and early scientific texts (up 18%). The demand for authenticated provenance has never been stronger — lots with full chain-of-ownership documentation commanded a 45% premium over equivalent lots without.

"The market is not growing indifferently. It is growing selectively. Collectors are more discerning than at any point in the last three decades." — Prof. James Alderton

Surprising Underperformers

Second and third editions — historically purchased as affordable proxies for first editions — declined 8% in value as collectors increasingly prioritise authenticity over access. Maps and atlases also softened after a period of strong growth, with buyer caution apparent in the mid-tier segment (£5,000–25,000).

Institutional Competition

University libraries, national archives, and foundations with significant endowments competed increasingly with private collectors at the top end of the market, pushing exceptional lots beyond private reach and into institutional ownership. This trend is expected to intensify through 2027.

Looking Ahead: 2026 Strategy

Our recommendation for 2026 is a continued focus on rigorous authentication, exceptional condition, and clear provenance. The market will continue to reward collectors who acquire with scholarly precision rather than speculative breadth. Category selection matters more than volume: a focused collection of 30 impeccably documented items will consistently outperform 200 poorly documented acquisitions.

As always, our curators are available to advise on specific acquisition strategies tailored to your collection and budget parameters.
