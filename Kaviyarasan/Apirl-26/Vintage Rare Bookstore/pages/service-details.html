Book Restoration & Conservation

Museum-grade techniques by trained conservators, using period-accurate materials consistent with ICON and Library of Congress standards.

Preserving the Physical Legacy

A rare book's condition is inseparable from its value — not merely monetary, but cultural and historical. Our master bookbinders trained in traditional crafts are supported by a conservation scientist who ensures all interventions are chemically stable and reversible.

We work with the finest period-appropriate materials: Japanese tissue for paper repair, hand-dyed Niger goatskin for rebinding, and alkyd gold size for tooled gilding. Every project is photographically documented before, during, and after treatment.

Our restoration philosophy is conservative: we do not exceed what is necessary to stabilise and present the object, and we always prioritise the long-term survival of original material over cosmetic improvement.

What's Included

  • Pre-treatment condition survey with photographic record
  • Microbiological assessment and pest exclusion
  • Aged leather humidification and reconsolidation
  • Spinal consolidation and endpaper renewal
  • Page deacidification and tear repair with Japanese tissue
  • Post-treatment care recommendations and custom clamshell box

Our Techniques

Restoration Specialisms

Leather Reconsolidation

Humidification, potassium lactate treatment, and Cellugel consolidant applied by brush — restoring suppleness without surface alteration.

Page Mending

Hand-applied Japanese tissue with wheat starch paste — the gold standard for archival paper repair, reversible under humidity.

Gold Tooling

Hand finishing with gold leaf and period-correct lettering tools, matched to original impressions using magnification and reference archives.

Custom Clamshell Boxes

Bespoke archival enclosures in book cloth, leather, or solander box format, providing pH-neutral protection for long-term storage.

Deacidification

Aqueous and non-aqueous deacidification treatments to neutralise paper acidity and extend the book's chemical lifespan by generations.

Photographic Records

Full before, during, and after macro photographic documentation provided with every project report for insurance and scholarship purposes.
