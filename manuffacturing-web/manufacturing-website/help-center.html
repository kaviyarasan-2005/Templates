Frequently Asked Questions

Can't find your answer? Contact our team directly.

Quoting & Orders

How do I request a quote?

You can request a quote by submitting your CAD files (STEP, IGES, DXF, or PDF drawings) through our contact form or emailing sales@forgecore.com. Include your quantity, required material, finish, and delivery date. We typically respond with a detailed quotation within 24 business hours.

What file formats do you accept for RFQ submission?

We accept STEP (.stp, .step), IGES (.igs, .iges), SOLIDWORKS (.sldprt), DXF (.dxf), PDF drawings, and AutoCAD (.dwg) files. For sheet metal, we prefer STEP or DXF with a flat pattern. 3D STEP files are preferred as they provide the most accurate manufacturing data.

What is your minimum order quantity (MOQ)?

MOQs vary by product type. CNC machined prototypes: 1 piece. Production machined parts: typically 25–50 pieces. Die castings: 500 pieces. Forgings: 100 pieces. Sheet metal fabrication: 10 pieces. Contact us for specific MOQ information for your part.

Do you offer blanket purchase orders?

Yes. We offer blanket purchase orders (BPOs) and vendor-managed inventory (VMI) programs for high-volume, repeat orders. BPOs allow you to lock in pricing for 12 months and schedule releases as needed, improving delivery predictability and reducing procurement overhead.

Quality & Certifications

What quality certifications do you hold?

We hold ISO 9001:2015 (Bureau Veritas), AS9100D (DNV), IATF 16949 (TÜV Rheinland), and NADCAP accreditation for non-destructive testing and heat treating. Copies of all current certificates are available upon request. Our quality management system is audited annually by accreditation bodies.

Do you provide first article inspection (FAI) reports?

Yes, we provide full AS9102 compliant First Article Inspection (FAI) reports as standard for all new part numbers and when there are significant process changes. The FAI includes all dimensional results, material certifications, process certifications, and functional test results.

Can you provide material traceability documentation?

Full material traceability is standard on all aerospace parts and available on request for other sectors. We maintain heat/lot number traceability from raw material mill certification through all processes to finished part. Material certifications (MTRs) are provided with every shipment.

Technical Questions

What tolerances can you achieve?

Our standard CNC machining tolerance is ±0.025mm (±0.001"). For precision-critical features, we can achieve ±0.005mm (±0.0002") on our high-precision grinding centers. Sheet metal forming: ±0.1mm. Die casting: ±0.1–0.3mm depending on feature. Please specify critical tolerances on your drawings.

What materials do you work with?

We machine, fabricate, and cast a wide range of metals: aluminum alloys (2024, 6061, 7075), titanium (Grade 2, Grade 5), stainless steel (303, 304, 316, 17-4PH), carbon steel, tool steel, Inconel 718, Hastelloy, copper alloys, and bronze. For plastics: Delrin, PEEK, Ultem, and nylon. Contact us if your material is not listed.

Do you offer design for manufacturability (DFM) reviews?

Yes, DFM reviews are included at no charge for all prototype orders and new part numbers. Our engineering team will review your design for machinability, feature accessibility, tolerance stack-up, and material selection, providing recommendations that can significantly reduce cost and lead time.

Delivery & Shipping

What are your standard lead times?

Prototype parts (1–10 pcs): 3–5 business days. Production runs (25–500 pcs): 3–4 weeks. Large production orders: 6–10 weeks depending on complexity. Rush services (48-hour) are available for prototype quantities at a surcharge. Lead times are confirmed at time of order placement.

Do you ship internationally?

Yes, we ship to 40+ countries worldwide. We handle all export documentation including commercial invoices, packing lists, certificates of origin, and EAR/ITAR export license applications where applicable. We work with DHL, FedEx International, and freight forwarders for large shipments.
